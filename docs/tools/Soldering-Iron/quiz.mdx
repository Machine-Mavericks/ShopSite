<DragDropQuiz name="Soldering">
<ol>
    <li>Ensure adequate <Blank answer="ventilation" />. Solder may contain lead. DO NOT breathe fumes from soldering or desoldering operations.</li>
    <li>Clamp work securely while performing soldering or desoldering. Use a vice or other approved clamping systems to keep your hands <Blank answer="free to work" />.</li>
    <li>Use heat sinks to protect circuit components. Do not apply excessive heat or apply heat for excessive <Blank answer="lengths of time" />.</li>
    <li>Beware of hot soldering irons and components. Make sure others are aware of hot tools. Switch off and/or <Blank answer="unplug" /> soldering tools when not in use. Allow to cool before storing.</li>
    <li>When working with used circuit boards, make sure desolder or solder area is clean and <Blank answer="free of dust" /> or grease before applying heat.</li>
    <li>Be aware of the <Blank answer="location" /> of your soldering iron or pencil while it is hot, or cooling. Make sure you place the hot iron in an appropriate holder to prevent heat or fire damage.</li>
    <li>When soldering wire connections, make sure the wires are tightly <Blank answer="connected" />. Use appropriate covering like heat shrink tubing or twist-on connectors to protect the splice. Do not use wires with melted insulation or exposed conductors.</li>
</ol>
